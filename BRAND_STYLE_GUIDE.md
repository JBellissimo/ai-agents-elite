## Typography

### Typefaces

**Display / Headlines**
- Font: **Cormorant Garamond** (Google Fonts, free)
- Weight: 600 (SemiBold) for headlines, 400 (Regular) for pull quotes
- Style: Slightly Italian, elegant, authoritative. Not stuffy.
- Use for: Page titles, section headers, report headings, the company name

**Body / UI**
- Font: **Inter** (Google Fonts, free)
- Weight: 400 (Regular) for body, 500 (Medium) for labels, 600 (SemiBold) for buttons
- Style: Clean, modern, highly readable at all sizes
- Use for: All body text, UI labels, navigation, descriptions

**Monospace / Technical**
- Font: **JetBrains Mono** (Google Fonts, free)
- Weight: 400 Regular
- Use for: Code snippets, agent output displays, technical specs, data tables

### Type Scale (Web)

| Role | Font | Size | Weight |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hero headline | Cormorant Garamond | 56â€“72px | 600 |
| Section header | Cormorant Garamond | 36â€“48px | 600 |

### Typography Rules
- Headlines: Title Case for marketing copy. Sentence case for reports and documents.
- Body text: Max line length of 70 characters. Short paragraphs. White space is content.
- Pull quotes: Cormorant Garamond, large, Gold color. One per section maximum.
- Numbers and data: JetBrains Mono. Always aligned right in tables.

## Voice & Tone

### Brand Voice: "The Expert Friend"
Imagine the most knowledgeable person you know who has no interest in impressing you â€”
only in being genuinely useful. They're direct. They tell you the uncomfortable truth
politely but clearly. They know what you should do next and they tell you.

### Writing Principles

**Be specific, never vague**
- Wrong: "AI can help your business."
- Right: "We automate the 8 hours/week your team spends on status reports."

**Earn every word**
- No filler phrases: "cutting-edge," "best-in-class," "leverage synergies," "disruptive"
- If a sentence doesn't add information or move the reader, delete it

**Lead with the insight, not the technology**
- Wrong: "Our AI agents use Claude's API to analyze your business data."
- Right: "We tell you what your business looks like through an AI lens â€” including the things your spreadsheets aren't showing you."

**Confident, not arrogant**
- Wrong: "Nobody else does this."
- Right: "Most companies are running on tools built before AI existed. We fix that."

**Warm but not casual**
- Wrong: "Hey! Super excited to chat about your biz! ðŸš€"
- Right: "Let's talk about what you're building. There may be more opportunity here than you think."

### Tone by Context

| Context | Tone |
|---|---|
| Marketing copy | Confident, intriguing, premium |
| The Reveal report | Expert, precise, slightly warm |
| Sales conversation | Curious, direct, no pressure |
| Email outreach | Brief, specific, value-first |
| Agent/product UI | Clear, helpful, efficient |

## Visual Design Principles

**1. White space is not empty â€” it's intentional**
Premium brands use restraint. Don't fill every pixel. Let content breathe.

**2. Data should be beautiful**
When we show numbers, charts, or reports â€” they should look like something worth framing.
Think Bloomberg Terminal meets a Michelin-starred menu.

**3. Maps as art**
The city map visual is a signature asset. It says: we see your world with precision
you don't have access to. Use it deliberately â€” not decoratively.

**4. No stock photo people**
Never use generic smiling-people-at-computers stock photos.
Use: maps, data visualizations, architectural photography, abstract geometry.

**5. Asymmetry over symmetry**
Layouts should feel considered, not templated. Offset columns.
Headlines that don't center. Margin notes. Visual tension.
